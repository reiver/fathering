Reading

learning to read

by Charles Iliya Krempeaux on Sunday June 7th, 2026

Recently I starting doing something more deliberate to continue to help my (5 year old) son learn to read.

I don't want to just rely on the schools he is going to. I want to help accelerate his learning.

Previously I was looking for something that takes a phonics and science of reading (SoR) approach to learning to read. So, two days ago I purchased Bob Books set 1 through set 5. They were delivered yesterday.

This morning I went through the first 3 books with my (5 year old) son.

He learned the sounds of some letters. He learned how to sound out words. He learned some three letter words. He said some simple sentences.

Before doing this, I saw others claim that at 5 years old a child's tolerance with their attention span tends to be limited. That was my experience with my (5 year old) son, too. He didn't want to do it anymore after 3 books.

My (5 year old) son did something that I didn't expect though. In the evening, he came and sat next to me, looked at the screen of my laptop, and started sounding out some of the words he saw 🙂 He was reading on his own! 🙂

I had both my (5 year old) son and (3 year old) daughter watch a few more alphablocks videos later in the evening, too.
