More Phonics

learning to read

by Charles Iliya Krempeaux on Saturday June 6th, 2026

My (5 year old) son and (3 year old) daugher:

  • both can sing the (English language) alphabet,
  • both can say the name of any (English language alphabet) letter if shown it,
  • both can write any letter of the (English language) alphabet,
  • both can write their own names (using the English language alphabet),
  • both can write some other things (using the English language alphabet) (such as each other's names, "MOMMY", "DADDY", "NANA", etc).

They have both been able to do these for a long time.

I started being more deliberate about helping my son learn to read. I started yesterday.

My son is now 5 years old, so I think he is probably ready for it now. (My 3 year old daughter will get some exposure to this, too, since they are often together.)

Yesterday I purchased Bob Books set 1 through set 5. I don't now much about them, but — I saw a recommendation for them as phonics based and science of reading (SoR) based tools for learning to read. (I suppose I will have an opinion about them in a number of years.)

Today I also had them watch a couple alphablocks videos. They also seems to be phonics based and science of reading (SoR) based tool for learning to read.

This isn't the first time they have been exposed to phonics. When my were children were younger they each watched a lot of Ms. Rachel! I think most parents (at the time of writing) know Ms. Rachel. Ms. Rachel is awesome! I noticed with both of my kids that their speech development accelerated after watching Ms. Rachel. Ms. Rachel uses science of reading (SoR) and phonics to help kids learn to speak and learn to read.
